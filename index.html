Abdul Sattar Edhi

1928-2016

The Early Years of Abdul Sattar Edhi

Abdul Sattar Edhi was born on February 28, 1928, in Bantva, British India (now in Gujarat, India). He migrated to Karachi after the partition of India in 1947. Edhi was deeply moved by the state of healthcare and social welfare in the country and took it upon himself to bring about change. At the age of 20, he started working towards his vision of a just society. With little formal education, Edhi learned from the struggles he witnessed and decided to dedicate his life to serving humanity.

The Birth of Edhi Foundation

In 1951, Edhi founded a small dispensary in Karachi. From these humble beginnings, he developed the Edhi Foundation into one of the largest and most respected charitable organizations in the world. The Foundation provides services such as:

  • Ambulance services (one of the largest fleet in the world)
  • Free hospitals and dispensaries
  • Orphanages and homes for the destitute
  • Educational services for the poor
  • Rehabilitation of drug addicts

    • His motto was simple: "Humanitarian work is beyond religion and race." This vision drove the foundation to serve millions without discrimination.

    Achievements of a Lifetime

    Over the decades, Abdul Sattar Edhi's work became synonymous with selflessness and dedication. His major milestones include:

  • Guinness World Record: The Edhi Foundation runs the largest volunteer ambulance service in the world.
  • Orphanages and Homes: Established hundreds of orphanages across Pakistan, giving abandoned children a second chance.
  • Global Recognition: Although Edhi refused many awards in his life, he was honored by governments, institutions, and the public around the world.

    • Quotes:

  • “I do not want to be remembered as anything other than someone who tried to be a human being.”
  • “People have become educated, but have yet to become human.”

    • Edhi's Philosophy and Vision

    Despite his massive contributions, Edhi lived a very simple life. He believed in practicing what he preached. His philosophy was grounded in the idea that all humans, regardless of background, should be treated with respect and dignity.

    Edhi advocated for the rights of the poor, the abandoned, and the forgotten. His mission to serve humanity continued even when his health deteriorated. He worked tirelessly until his last days, inspiring millions of people globally to give back to their communities.

    The Lasting Legacy of Abdul Sattar Edhi

    Abdul Sattar Edhi passed away on July 8, 2016, at the age of 88. His death was mourned not only in Pakistan but across the world. Today, the Edhi Foundation is run by his family, particularly his son, Faisal Edhi, and it continues to grow and evolve, serving those in need.

    Edhi's legacy lives on through the continued work of the Foundation and the millions who were inspired by his life of service. His life's work remains a testament to what one individual, driven by compassion and humanity, can achieve.
